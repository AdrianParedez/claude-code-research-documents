Research frame

The page now behaves more like a finding aid than a software landing page.

The design is more lavish now, but the writing rule is the same: say what is known, say what is inferred, and show the reader why the confidence level differs.

Scope

Question-first framing

Each paper starts from a bounded question: what prompt architecture works, what system Claude Code has become, and what evidence can support those claims without hand-waving.

Evidence

Primary sources first

Current official docs, engineering posts, and product materials are treated as the strongest practical sources when the product behavior changes quickly.

Discipline

Date clarity matters

Exact dates such as February 24, 2025 and March 25, 2026 are part of the method because relative phrasing hides version drift in fast-moving tools.

Confidence ladder

Mechanical facts should outrank directional storytelling.

This is the papers’ core evidentiary rule translated into the interface itself.

  1. High confidence Documented surfaces, settings, permissions, workflows, and architecture.
  2. Moderate confidence Operational effects with first-party evidence and explicit caveats.
  3. Low confidence Superiority claims without matched independent benchmarks.
